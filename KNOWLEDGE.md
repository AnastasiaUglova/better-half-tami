# Knowledge base — sources & accuracy notes

The substance SAGE and Jedi Master C-3PO teach from. The condensed, runtime version
lives in `KNOWLEDGE_BASE` in `netlify/functions/chat.js`; this file is the fuller,
sourced reference. Researched June 2026. **Rules of thumb are flagged — don't overstate.**

---

## 1. The defended self (ego) & social rupture

**Core:** Criticism lands as danger because the mind blurs "you got this wrong" into "you
are bad/unlovable." People then protect the self-image instead of the problem — and that
self-protection (defensiveness) is what ruptures connection.

- **Self-affirmation theory** (Claude Steele, 1988): we defend global self-integrity under
  threat unless worth is anchored elsewhere.
- **Shame vs. guilt** (Brené Brown): guilt = "I did something bad"; shame = "I am bad."
  Shame drives blame and disconnection.
- **Gottman's Four Horsemen:** criticism (character attack), contempt (mockery/superiority
  — most corrosive), defensiveness (excuses/counter-attack), stonewalling (shutdown, usually
  after flooding). Antidote to defensiveness: take responsibility for even 2%.
- **Rupture & repair** (Ed Tronick, Still-Face): bonds are built by *repairing* rupture, not
  avoiding it.

**⚠ Accuracy:** the widely-cited "90%+ divorce-prediction accuracy" stat is **disputed**
(post-hoc/retrospective models, criticized by statisticians e.g. Heyman) — omitted.
"Contempt is the strongest single predictor" is the safer, supported claim. "Heart rate
>100 bpm" is a popularized rule of thumb. Tronick's "~70% mismatch" is from infant–caregiver
studies; extending to adult romance is interpretive.

Sources: Steele 1988; Gottman Institute (Four Horsemen); Tronick (MINDinMIND legacy interview);
shame summaries (Brown).

## 2. Nonviolent Communication (NVC — Marshall Rosenberg)

**OFNR model:** Observation (camera-factual, no interpretation) → Feeling (genuine emotion) →
Need (universal need underneath) → Request (specific, doable, survives a "no").

**Key distinctions:** observation ≠ evaluation; feeling ≠ thought (faux-feelings:
"ignored / disrespected / manipulated" are thoughts — test: if "that" fits after "I feel,"
it's a thought); need ≠ strategy (conflict lives at strategy level; needs rarely conflict);
request ≠ demand (if "no" isn't allowed, it's a demand). "Jackal" (blame) vs "giraffe"
(needs-based) language. Empathy + self-empathy interrupt escalation.

**⚠ Pitfalls:** "robot NVC" (rigid recitation breaks connection); using it to manipulate;
pseudo-empathy; doesn't resolve power/trauma imbalances. Treat OFNR as scaffolding, not script.
The 9-class needs taxonomy is Max-Neef's (Rosenberg drew on it); NVC doesn't mandate a fixed list.

Sources: cnvc.org; BayNVC; Rosenberg; Oren Jay Sofer (pitfalls); Wikipedia: Nonviolent Communication.

## 3. Emotional triage & flooding

**Core:** the nervous system comes before the content. A flooded brain can't reason, hear, or
empathize, so discussing/problem-solving while activated fails or escalates. Stabilize first,
sort fault later.

- **Flooding / diffuse physiological arousal** (Gottman): sympathetic fight/flight; prefrontal
  cortex partly offline. ~**100 bpm** sustained is the cited threshold.
- **~20-minute** recovery break — but only if you stop ruminating. Break ≥20 min and ≤24 hrs.
- **Good time-out (repair, not abandonment):** name it → signal care → set a return time →
  self-soothe (don't rehearse the case). Silent exit = stonewalling.
- **Resume** when you can hold the other's perspective without your jaw clenching.

**⚠ Accuracy:** 100 bpm / 20 min are **clinical heuristics**, not universal constants. The
ER/battlefield-medic "triage" framing is a pedagogical analogy (ours), well-paired with the
empirically stronger co-regulation literature.

Sources: Gottman Institute (flooding/DPA, stonewalling, self-soothe); polyvagal & co-regulation
in conflict.

## 4. Co-regulation & attunement

**Co-regulation:** two nervous systems stabilize (or alarm) each other through tone, facial
expression, pace, breath. Developmental root: caregiver–infant; never outgrown. Safety is built
relationally, before content lands. You can only lend a calm you have.

**Attunement** (Stern's affect attunement; Siegel's "feeling felt"): sensing and resonating with
another's *internal* state and responding contingently. Attune to the **feeling, not the
position** — resonance ≠ agreement, ≠ mimicry.

- **Cues of safety vs. threat** (polyvagal framing): warm prosody/soft eyes/unhurried pace = safe
  & social; sharp/fast/scanning = fight-flight; numb/collapsed = shutdown. Tone & pacing matter
  more than word content.
- **Moves:** regulate yourself first; prosody over content; match-then-lead (over-slowing
  patronizes, over-mirroring mocks); slow the pace; reflect ("name to tame"); ask "what would help?"
- **Rupture & repair** (Tronick): dyads mismatch ~60–70% of the time; resilience comes from repair,
  not flawless attunement.

**⚠ Accuracy:** **Polyvagal Theory is clinically influential but scientifically contested**
(Grossman; Monteiro et al. 2018 challenge the evolutionary premises). Treat the *practices*
(prosody, pacing, co-regulation) as well-grounded; treat the *neuro-evolutionary mechanism* as a
contested model, not settled fact.

**Neurodivergent note:** neuroception thresholds differ; safety often needs *predictability*
(what happens next) more than warm improvisation; some don't transmit typical cues yet feel
deeply — make internal states explicit, offer predictability, ask rather than infer.

Sources: Porges (Polyvagal Institute; Frontiers 2022); Stern; Siegel; Tronick (*The Power of
Discord*); Grossman et al. (critique); Wikipedia: Polyvagal theory (criticism).

## 5. AuDHD social reality (esp. reading cues)

**Core framing — not a one-sided deficit:** the **double empathy problem** (Damian Milton, 2012)
— breakdowns between autistic and non-autistic people are *mutual and bidirectional*.
Empirically supported: **Crompton et al. 2020** — autistic-to-autistic info transfer is as
effective as neurotypical-to-neurotypical; rapport drops *specifically in mixed-neurotype pairs*.
Lead with this.

- **Cue-reading** goes both ways: AuDHD adults may miss/misweight others' tone/subtext, *and* are
  themselves misread (flat prosody, atypical eye contact read as cold/disengaged).
- **Alexithymia** (difficulty identifying one's own emotions; ~50–60% of autistic people) +
  **interoception** differences → may not register own/others' rising activation until acute.
  **⚠** Shah et al. 2016: interoceptive difficulty tracks with *co-occurring alexithymia*, not
  autism per se — don't over-attribute "not noticing feelings" to "being autistic."
- **RSD (rejection sensitive dysphoria):** criticism lands as intense, near-physical pain.
  **⚠ Informal clinical-community construct (Dodson), NOT a DSM-5 diagnosis** — present as a
  lived-experience frame, not a diagnosis. The intensity is real.
- **Masking/camouflaging** is exhausting and burns the reserves needed for repair.
- **Monotropism** (attention tunnels): off-tunnel social signals become "less real."

**What a coach does differently:** be explicit not implicit (make the unspoken spoken);
externalize interoception ("where do you feel that?" over "how do you feel?"); strengths-based
(honor the logic/fixing instinct, add the emotional layer beside it); pre-empt RSD (collaborative,
specific, separate behavior from person); frame conflict as mutual translation, reducing shame.

Sources: Milton 2012 & 2022; Crompton et al. 2020 (*Autism*); Shah et al. 2016 (PMC); Hull et al.
(camouflaging); Dodson / Neurodivergent Insights (RSD); Murray, Lesser & Lawson 2005 (monotropism);
National Autistic Society.
